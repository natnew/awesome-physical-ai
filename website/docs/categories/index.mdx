# Categories

The Awesome Physical AI list is organised into **14 canonical categories**. Each category mirrors the corresponding section of the root [`README.md`](https://github.com/natnew/awesome-physical-ai/blob/main/README.md) — the README remains the source of truth. Each category page now opens with a short engineering-oriented intro covering what it is, why it matters for deployment, and how to choose between entries.

- [Simulators](./simulators.mdx) — Physics engines and rendering stacks where policies are trained and stress-tested before touching hardware.
- [Datasets](./datasets.mdx) — Recorded robot behaviour — teleoperation, demonstrations, egocentric video — that feeds imitation learning and pretraining.
- [Benchmarks](./benchmarks.mdx) — Fixed task suites for comparing policies under controlled conditions across manipulation, locomotion, and embodied reasoning.
- [Evaluation Methodology](./evaluation-methodology.mdx) — How to measure policies *correctly* — harnesses, metrics, and statistical practice that turn rollouts into defensible claims.
- [Robotics Foundation Models](./robotics-foundation-models.mdx) — Generalist pretrained policies and vision-language-action (VLA) models that map perception and language to robot actions.
- [World Models](./world-models.mdx) — Learned predictive models of physical dynamics used for imagination-based planning, sample-efficient RL, and synthetic data.
- [Manipulation](./manipulation.mdx) — Grasping, dexterous, and contact-rich methods — analytic planners, diffusion and transformer policies, and the rigs that feed them.
- [Locomotion](./locomotion.mdx) — Legged, bipedal, and humanoid controllers and the GPU-parallel training stacks that make modern sim-to-real practical.
- [Sim-to-Real](./sim-to-real.mdx) — Techniques for closing the reality gap — domain randomisation, system identification, residual learning, and real-robot fine-tuning.
- [Safety & Robustness](./safety-and-robustness.mdx) — Constrained training, adversarial evaluation, and failure-mode analysis that decide whether a system is shippable.
- [Governance & Policy](./governance-and-policy.mdx) — Standards, regulation, and risk frameworks that shape how Physical AI systems are evaluated and deployed.
- [Production Patterns / Reference Architectures](./production-patterns.mdx) — Middleware, planners, fleet orchestration, and observability for shipping robots in production.
- [Courses](./courses.mdx) — Structured learning paths into robot learning and embodied AI for individuals and team onboarding.
- [Companies](./companies.mdx) — Organisations actively shaping Physical AI in industry — foundation-model labs, platform builders, and applied deployers.
